The AI Ops Manager Community

For the people that
AI systems depend on.

The AI Ops Manager is one of the most consequential emerging roles in modern organizations. The person who stays close enough to notice when something drifts. Who holds the relationship between the system and the people it serves. Who carries the knowledge of how it all fits together — often without a job title, a peer group, or a playbook to work from.

This community exists for that person. To build the playbook together, share what's working across organizations, and give the people doing this work the peers they deserve.

A New Kind of Role

The most important person in an AI-powered organization rarely has a title (yet.)

The AI Ops Manager sits at the intersection of operations, governance, and AI literacy. They notice changes before they become problems. They maintain the living relationship between the system and the people it serves. They carry the full picture — the dependencies, the risks, the fixes, the context that nobody else holds.

Most organizations have someone doing this work already. They picked it up alongside another role, or stepped into it because nobody else did. They're figuring out the boundaries of the job in real time, usually without peers who understand what it actually involves.

This community is where that changes.

The Right Fit

Three ways people arrive here.

Drawn to the frontier

Operating at the edge of AI implementation, agentic systems, or organizational design — and looking for the community doing serious work in this space.

Developing into the role

Moving through an ST:AI transformation engagement and building the capability to own and run the systems being built for your organization.

Already in the role

Running AI systems inside an organization and looking for peers, patterns, and a community that understands what the work actually involves.

The Community in Motion

Active, open, and growing.

The community lives primarily on Discord, with regular structured sessions, open channels for daily practice sharing, and working groups forming around specific domains and challenges.

Starting points for new members: introduce yourself, share what you're running, ask the question you've been sitting on. The community is at its best when people arrive with real work.

The Bigger Picture

This community sits within SmokingTigers.Enterprises — a cooperative built around the idea that the people doing the most consequential work in the emerging AI economy deserve peers, infrastructure, and a stake in what gets built.

The AI Ops Manager role is central to how ST:AI works with client organizations. The community is where the people carrying that role — across organizations, sectors, and stages — come together to build something more durable than any single implementation.

The standards, patterns, and practices that emerge here feed back into everything ST:AI builds. Better practice in the community means better systems in the organizations means stronger evidence of what's actually possible.
